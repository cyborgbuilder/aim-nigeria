Conference Overview

Nigeria’s oil and gas industry has long been the foundation of the nation’s economy, accounting for over 90% of export value and 5.5% of GDP. With over 300 developed fields, 265 processing stations, and thousands of kilometers of pipelines feeding into 31 export terminals, the country remains a strategic energy player in Africa and globally.

But Nigeria’s energy infrastructure is aging. Corrosion is increasing. Pipeline failures and unplanned shutdowns are becoming more frequent—costing millions in lost production, environmental risk, and reputational damage. At the same time, new regulatory requirements under the Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) demand higher standards of asset integrity, compliance, and safety performance.

Many operators lack the systems, tools, and skilled workforce to respond to this moment.

The Asset Integrity & Corrosion Management Conference – Nigeria 2025 arrives at a critical turning point. Designed to support the nation’s upstream and midstream stakeholders, the event brings together regulators, operators, EPCs, and technology providers to share strategies for improving reliability, reducing downtime, and future-proofing critical assets. From corrosion control and pipeline protection to predictive maintenance and digital integrity tools, this conference delivers actionable insights Nigeria’s energy sector urgently needs—right now.

Strategic Integrity Challenges

  • Pipeline Corrosion & Security Risk

    Crude theft and sabotage in Niger Delta; poor leak detection, response systems

  • FPSO Aging & Offshore Fatigue

    Mooring system fatigue, corrosion, asset life beyond design life

  • Refinery & Gas Plant Maintenance Gaps

    Irregular turnarounds, severe corrosion in legacy systems

  • Low Digital Adoption

    Minimal use of real-time monitoring, digital twins, or AI-powered inspection

  • Workforce Competency Gaps

    Hidden corrosion beneath insulation, often undetected until failure occurs

  • FRP Technology & Material Challenges

    Failure in FRP due to poor specs, UV degradation, or incompatible process conditions

Sector Landscape & Market Context

  • Nigeria is Africa’s largest oil producer, home to vast upstream, gas, and LNG reserves.
  • Its energy sector suffers from:
    • Decades-old infrastructure
    • Pipeline sabotage
    • FPSO fatigue
    • Chronic turnaround delays
  • The Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) was passed in 2021 to reform governance and performance, but real implementation remains inconsistent.
  • Integrity failures have led to repeated shutdowns, oil spills, production losses, and investor hesitation.
