Conference Overview

Nigeria’s oil and centre assets are aging, corrosion is rising, and unplanned shutdowns are costing millions. With stricter regulations under the PIA and growing pressure on operators to improve safety, performance, and compliance, the industry is at a turning point.

Most companies lack the systems, technology, or skilled workforce to manage asset integrity effectively.

The Asset Integrity & Corrosion Management Conference – Nigeria 2025 arrives at a critical moment.

Bringing together regulators, operators, EPCs, and solution providers, this event delivers real strategies to extend asset life, prevent failures, and stay ahead of compliance. From corrosion control to predictive maintenance, this conference provides the tools and insights Nigeria’s energy industry needs—right now.

Key challenges impacting Nigeria's energy sector integrity and performance.

Challenge
Description
Pipeline Corrosion & Security Risk
Crude theft and sabotage in Niger Delta; poor leak detection, response systems

Low Digital Adoption

Hidden corrosion beneath insulation, often undetected until failure occurs, especially in coastal/humid zones

Sector Landscape & Market Context

  • Nigeria is Africa’s largest oil producer, home to vast upstream, gas, and LNG reserves.
  • Its energy sector suffers from:
    • Decades-old infrastructure
    • Pipeline sabotage
    • FPSO fatigue
    • Chronic turnaround delays
  • The Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) was passed in 2021 to reform governance and performance, but real implementation remains inconsistent.
  • Integrity failures have led to repeated shutdowns, oil spills, production losses, and investor hesitation.
